THE COMPONENTS INSIDE FUEL CELLS
In a fuel cell, there are four major components: an anode, a proton exchange
membrane(PEM), a cathode, and the bipolar plates.
Gas diffusion layer

facilitate gas/water transport between gas
flow channel and catalyst layer pore; conduct
electron between electrode and bipolar plate;
load distribution in MEA.
MAIN MENU
Bipolar plates

Provide electrical conduction between
cells; provide physical strength to the
stack; and act as flow plates to allow
gases to flow over the MEA.
Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM)

Provide rapid transport of protons from
anode to cathode; act as barrier of
gases and electrons; provide rapid
transport of water.

Catalyst layer

Cathode electrode: reduce oxygen to
water with protons transported;
resistant to degradation due to start/
stop and duty cycle.
Catalyst layer

Anode electrode: oxidize hydrogen to
protons; resistant to degradation due
to fuel starvation.
